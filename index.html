About Me

Thomas Lim, Cellist

New York City based cellist, performer, and artist.

Named a National YoungArts Foundation Winner in 2019, Korean-American Cellist Thomas Lim is the recipient of numerous awards including first prize at the MTAC VOCE Competition, the Korea Times Youth Music Competition, the Diablo Symphony Orchestra Yen Liang Concerto Competition, and has been a prize winner in competitions including the 2022 Juilliard Concerto Competition, the New York International Artists Association Cello Competition, the Montgomery Symphony Orchestra Blount-Slawson Young Artists' Competition, the Lewisville Lake Symphony Orchestra Concerto Competition, the Coeur d'Alene Concerto Competition, and the Nova Vista Symphony Orchestra Concerto Competition.

Thomas has performed in masterclasses for and worked with globally esteemed artists including Lynn Harrell, Frans Helmerson, Laurence Lesser, Joel Krosnick, Hans Jorgen Jensen, Luis Claret, David Finckel, Fred Sherry, Gilbert Kalish, Soovin Kim, Jerome Lowenthal, Nicholas Mann, Joseph Lin, Daniel Phillips, Patricia McCarty, and members of the American, Tokyo, Orion, Juilliard, Schumann, Formosa, Escher, and Alexander String Quartets. Over the summers, Thomas has made appearances at Music@Menlo, Kneisel Hall, Meadowmount School of Music, and Orford Musique and has previously studied with Jonathan Koh (SFCM) and Minja Hyun (Yonsei University).

Thomas graduated Magna cum Laude from Columbia University with a Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science and Mathematics while in the Columbia-Juilliard exchange program. Currently, he is an M.M. candidate at the Juilliard School studying with Darrett Adkins and Natasha Brofsky.

Teaching

Learn more about my teaching

I have over four years of experience teaching students of all ages and levels since 2018, with primary focus on students ages 9-16. With the right mentor, anyone can learn to play the cello and learn to love music. I offer lessons in person in New York, or online, and strive to create a dynamic, engaging learning enviornment in any teaching modality.

For each student, I personally craft a curriculum consiting of repertoire, technique, etudes, theory, and more.

Students may expect to use the any of the following books, depending on level:
Suzuki Cello School
Feuillard: Méthode du Jeune Violoncelliste
Duport: 21 Exercises
Grutzmacher: 24 Etudes
Jensen: Fun in Thumb Position
Galamian-Jensen: Scale System
Jensen: CelloMind
Yampolsky: Violoncello Technique
Dotzauer: 113 Etudes
Popper: 40 Etudes

Whether you are just starting out on the cello, or want to improve your skills, I can help you achieve your goals!
