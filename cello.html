Thomas Lim

Cellist

Biography

Named a National YoungArts Foundation Winner in 2019, Korean-American Cellist Thomas Lim is the recipient of numerous awards including first prize at the MTAC VOCE Competition, the Korea Times Youth Music Competition, the Diablo Symphony Orchestra Yen Liang Concerto Competition, and has been a prize winner in competitions including the New York International Artists Association Cello Competition, the Montgomery Symphony Orchestra Blount-Slawson Young Artistsâ€™ Competition, and the Nova Vista Symphony Orchestra Concerto Competition.

Thomas has worked with esteemed artists such as Lynn Harrell, Hans Jorgen Jensen, Gilbert Kalish, David Finckel, Patricia McCarty, Soovin Kim, and members of the American, Tokyo, Orion, Juilliard, Schumann, Formosa, Escher, and Alexander String Quartets. Thomas has previously studied with Jonathan Koh and Minja Hyun. Currently an undergraduate at Columbia University in the City of New York, Thomas studies with Darrett Adkins at the Juilliard School as part of the Columbia-Juilliard Exchange Program.

Cellist Thomas Lim currently studies with Darrett Adkins at the Juilliard School through the Columbia-Juilliard exchange program. Named a National YoungArts Foundation Winner in 2019 and 2020, Korean-American Cellist Thomas Lim is the recipient of numerous awards including first prize at the Korea Times Youth Music Competition and the Diablo Symphony Orchestra Concerto Competition. Thomas has worked with esteemed artists including Lynn Harrell, Laurence Lesser, Hans Jorgen Jensen, Lluis Claret, Gilbert Kalish, David Finckel, Patricia McCarty, Soovin Kim, and members of the American, Tokyo, Orion, Juilliard, Emerson, Schumann, Formosa, Escher, and Alexander String Quartets. Thomas has made appearances at the Meadowmount School of Music, Music@Menlo, and Orford Musique, and has previously studied with Jonathan Koh and Minja Hyun. Thomas also enjoys spending his time playing sports, cooking, and listening to music. At Columbia, Thomas is an undergraduate student studying Computer Science and Mathematics.
